2025/05/23

Deriving the Distribution of \(\hat{\beta}_0\)

Assume that \(Y_i \sim N(\beta_0 + \beta_1 X_i, \sigma^2)\) as a result of the definition
\(Y_i = \beta_0 + \beta_1 X_i + \varepsilon_i\), where each \(\varepsilon_i \sim N(0, \sigma^2)\).
This is what’s assumed under the simple linear regression model.

We treat the \(X_i\)’s as fixed. \(Y\) is treated as a random variable, owing to the error term.

\[ \hat{\beta}_0 = \bar{Y} - \hat{\beta}_1 \bar{X} \]

Since \(\hat{\beta}_1 \sim N\left(\beta_1, \frac{\sigma^2}{S_{xx}}\right)\), and \(\bar{X}\) is a constant determined by the sample and \(\bar{Y}\) is normally distributed, \(\hat{\beta}_0\) is normally distributed.

\[ `\begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}[\hat{\beta}_0] &= \mathbb{E}[\bar{Y}] - \mathbb{E}[\hat{\beta}_1 \bar{X}] \\ &= \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{1}{n} \sum (\beta_0 + \beta_1 X_i)\right] - \mathbb{E}[\hat{\beta}_1 \bar{X}] \\ &= \mathbb{E}\left[\beta_0 + \beta_1 \bar{X} \right] - \bar{X} \cdot \mathbb{E}[\hat{\beta}_1] \\ &= \beta_0 + \beta_1 \bar{X} - \beta_1 \bar{X} \\ &= \beta_0 \end{aligned}` \]\[ `\begin{aligned} \operatorname{Var}(\hat{\beta}_0) &= \operatorname{Var}(\bar{Y} - \bar{X} \hat{\beta}_1) \\ &= \operatorname{Var}(\bar{Y}) + \bar{X}^2 \operatorname{Var}(\hat{\beta}_1) - 2 \bar{X} \operatorname{Cov}(\bar{Y}, \hat{\beta}_1) \\ &= \sigma^2 \left( \frac{1}{n} \right) + \bar{X}^2 \cdot \frac{\sigma^2}{S_{xx}} \quad \text{(since } \operatorname{Cov}(\bar{Y}, \hat{\beta}_1) = 0\text{)} \\ &= \sigma^2 \left[ \frac{1}{n} + \frac{\bar{X}^2}{S_{xx}} \right] \end{aligned}` \]

Thus, we arrive at

\[ \hat{\beta}_0 \sim N\left( \beta_0,\ \sigma^2 \left[ \frac{1}{n} + \frac{\bar{X}^2}{S_{xx}} \right] \right) \]
